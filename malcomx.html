Malcolm X

1925 - 1965

The Defender of Human Rights

Malcolm X , original name Malcolm Little, Muslim name el-Hajj Malik el-Shabazz, (born May 19, 1925, Omaha, Nebraska, U.S.—died February 21, 1965, New York, New York), African American leader and prominent figure in the Nation of Islam who articulated concepts of race pride and Black nationalism in the early 1960s. After his assassination, the widespread distribution of his life story— The Autobiography of Malcolm X (1965)—made him an ideological hero, especially among Black youth.

The following list is a time line of Malcolm X's Life

  • May 19 1925 - Malcolm X is born Malcolm Little in Omaha, Nebraka
  • 1926 - The Littles leave Omaha and move to Milwaukee, Wisconsin.
  • 1928 - The Littles leave Milwaukee and move to Lansing, Michigan.
  • Sep 28 1931 - Earl Little (Malcom X's father) fatally struck by streetcar
  • 1939 - Malcolm X drops out of school after 8th grade
  • 1943 - Malcolm X is ordered to register for military
  • Jan 12 1946 - Malcolm X is arrested for burglary
  • 1952 - Malcolm Little changes surname to "X"
  • Aug 7 1952 - Malcolm X is paroled and released from prison
  • Jan 14 1958 - Malcolm X marries Betty Sanders
  • Feb 1964 to Feb 1965 - The Nation of Islam make threats against Malcolm X
  • Mar 8 1964 - Malcolm X announces his break from the Nation of Islam
  • Mar 26 1964 - Malcolm X meets with Martin Luther King Jr.
  • Apr 13 1964 to Feb 5 1965 - Malcolm X's International Travel
  • Feb 14 1965 - Malcolm X's house is firebombed
  • Feb 21 1965 - Malcolm X Assassinated in Manhattan's Audubon Ballroom
  • Feb 23 1965 to Feb 27 1965 - Malcolm X's Funeral
